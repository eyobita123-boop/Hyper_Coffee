Our story

From Yirgacheffe to your table

We work directly with smallholder growers in Yirgacheffe and Sidamo, roasting in small batches a few times a week. Our baristas are trained in both the espresso bar and the traditional ceremony — because we think a city café can hold both.

How we pour

The three rounds

In a traditional ceremony, the same beans are brewed three times, each round a little lighter and a little gentler than the last. We use the same idea to structure how we brew, serve, and pace your visit.

አቦል Abol — first round

The strongest pour

Freshly roasted beans, ground moments before brewing. Bold, concentrated, served to set the tone — this is the cup that wakes the room up.

ጦና Tona — second round

The conversation cup

Same grounds, hot water added again. Softer, rounder, meant for lingering — most of our regulars order their second cup exactly here.

በረካ Baraka — third round

The blessing

Light, gentle, and traditionally the cup of good fortune. We pour it on the house for anyone who's stayed for all three rounds.
